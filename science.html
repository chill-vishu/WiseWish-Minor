What is Science?

Science is a systematic approach to understanding the natural world through observation, experimentation, and analysis. It involves asking questions, forming hypotheses, and testing them to draw conclusions about how things work. Relying on empirical evidence, science emphasizes reproducibility and the ability to falsify hypotheses. It encompasses various branches, including natural sciences (like physics and biology), formal sciences (like mathematics), social sciences (like psychology), and applied sciences (like engineering). By enhancing our knowledge and fostering critical thinking, science drives innovations that improve our lives and help us tackle global challenges.
